Economic Outlook 2025: Analysis and Projections

Chişinău As 2025 begins, Moldova's economic prospects are once again in the spotlight, with economists, policymakers, and international institutions offering analysis and forecasts for the year ahead. Following several years marked by global uncertainty, energy price shocks, and regional instability, attention is now focused on whether Moldova can maintain growth, control inflation, and sustain progress toward long-term economic stability. Performance in 2024 Moldova's economy ended 2024 on a cautiously positive note, showing signs of recovery after periods of volatility linked to the war in Ukraine, fluctuations in global energy markets, and supply chain disruptions. Preliminary data from the National Bureau of Statistics indicates that GDP growth reached approximately 3.5%, supported by strong agricultural output and moderate increases in services and construction. However, industrial production remained uneven, reflecting the ongoing challenges faced by exporters in accessing regional markets. Remittances from Moldovans working abroad continued to play a critical role, accounting for more than 10% of GDP, though experts warn that reliance on remittances exposes the country to external shocks. Inflation Trends Inflation has been one of the most closely monitored indicators. After peaking at double-digit levels in 2022, consumer prices began to stabilize in 2023 and slowed further throughout 2024. By December, annual inflation had eased to just under 6%, placing it within the National Bank of Moldova's target range. For 2025, economists expect inflation to remain relatively stable, with forecasts ranging between 5% and 6%. Much will depend on energy costs, food prices, and exchange rate dynamics. "Inflationary pressures have eased significantly, but Moldova remains vulnerable to external price shocks, particularly in energy imports," explained a senior analyst at a leading Chişinău-based think tank. Growth Projections for 2025 The World Bank, International Monetary Fund (IMF), and the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) have all published forecasts for Moldova in 2025. Projections vary slightly but generally fall in the range of 3.5% to 4.2% GDP growth. The main drivers are expected to be agriculture, supported by favorable weather conditions, and the service sector, particularly IT and digital exports, which continue to expand. Government investment in infrastructure projects is also anticipated to provide a boost, with road rehabilitation and energy efficiency programs high on the agenda. Nevertheless, risks remain. "Moldova's growth outlook is positive, but fragile," cautioned an IMF representative. "Geopolitical tensions, energy dependency, and structural weaknesses in the business environment continue to pose challenges." Labor Market and Wages Employment levels in 2024 showed slight improvement, particularly in urban areas, though rural regions continued to face higher unemployment. Labor shortages in certain sectors, especially construction and agriculture, are expected to persist into 2025. Wages grew by an average of 10% in nominal terms last year, though real wage growth was more modest once inflation was taken into account. The government has announced plans to raise public sector wages, particularly for teachers and healthcare workers, in 2025. Economists note that wage growth, if not matched by productivity gains, could put renewed pressure on inflation. Fiscal and Monetary Policy On the fiscal side, Moldova has maintained relative discipline, with a budget deficit projected at around 4% of GDP for 2024. The government plans to continue targeted social support programs, particularly subsidies for vulnerable households facing high energy bills. At the same time, authorities have pledged to gradually reduce the deficit to ensure debt sustainability. The National Bank of Moldova is expected to maintain a cautious monetary policy stance. Interest rates were lowered slightly in late 2024 to encourage investment, but central bankers emphasize that stability remains their primary objective. Key Sectors to Watch Agriculture – Still a cornerstone of Moldova's economy, agriculture is expected to contribute significantly in 2025, though climate-related risks remain. IT and Services – Digital industries continue to expand, with IT exports exceeding $400 million in 2024. Economists see this sector as a key driver of future growth. Energy – Diversification of energy imports remains a top priority. Recent investments in renewable energy projects are expected to reduce reliance on external suppliers, though progress will be gradual. Wine Industry – Moldova's traditional wine exports rebounded modestly in 2024, supported by growing access to European markets. Further expansion in 2025 will depend on marketing and international trade conditions. Challenges and Risks Despite positive signs, Moldova faces several structural challenges. The business environment, while improving, continues to struggle with regulatory complexity and limited access to financing for small and medium-sized enterprises. Demographic trends pose another long-term issue, as migration and declining birth rates reduce the labor force. Externally, Moldova remains highly exposed to regional instability. The ongoing conflict in neighboring Ukraine continues to weigh on trade, investment confidence, and energy security. Any escalation could have immediate repercussions for Moldova's economic stability. Outlook and Conclusion Overall, Moldova enters 2025 with cautious optimism. Growth is expected to remain steady, inflation appears largely under control, and structural reforms are gradually taking shape. Yet the country's economic trajectory will depend heavily on external conditions, government policy choices, and the ability to implement reforms effectively. Economists agree that the year ahead presents both opportunities and risks. If favorable conditions hold, Moldova could strengthen its position as a growing European economy with increasing integration into regional markets. If shocks occur, however, vulnerabilities in energy supply, trade, and demographics could once again come to the forefront. For now, policymakers are focused on balancing stability with reform. As one economist summed up during a recent conference in Chişinău: "Moldova has the chance to consolidate its recovery in 2025, but the margin for error is small. Careful management will be crucial."

Context & Analysis

The National Bank of Moldova has released its latest economic forecast, projecting moderate growth of 3.2% for 2025.

Economic Highlights:

  • GDP growth projected at 3.2% for 2025
  • Inflation expected to stabilize around 5.5%
  • Unemployment rates show promising downward trends
  • Agricultural exports increased by 12% this quarter

Key factors driving this growth include increased foreign investment, robust agricultural production, and continued expansion of the technology sector. The IT industry in particular has shown remarkable growth, with exports increasing by 18% compared to last year.

Economic Analyst's View

"What we're seeing is a carefully balanced economic recovery. While inflation remains a concern, the central bank's monetary policies appear to be having the desired effect. The tech sector's performance is particularly encouraging - it suggests Moldova is successfully diversifying its economy beyond traditional sectors."

- Andrei Volkov, Economic Analyst

Sector Performance

The agriculture sector continues to be a strong performer, with wine exports reaching record levels. Meanwhile, the manufacturing sector shows signs of recovery, particularly in textile production and automotive components.

Tourism has also seen significant growth, with visitor numbers up 25% compared to the same period last year, driven by increased promotion of Moldova's cultural heritage and wine tourism.
