Local Businesses Report Optimism Amid Economic Recovery

Confidence index reaches highest level in five years as entrepreneurship flourishes

Entrepreneurial Spirit Revitalized

Chișinău - Moldovan business owners are expressing unprecedented optimism about economic prospects, according to the latest National Business Confidence Survey released today. The index reached 78.5 points, its highest level since 2020, indicating strong confidence in the country's economic direction.

The survey of 1,200 businesses across various sectors shows particular strength in retail, technology services, and food processing. Small and medium enterprises (SMEs) reported the most significant improvement in outlook, with 68% expecting to expand their operations in the next six months.

"The combination of government support programs, access to EU markets, and growing domestic consumption has created ideal conditions for business growth," explained Mihai Gheorghiu, owner of a successful local manufacturing company. "We're hiring new staff and investing in equipment for the first time in years."

The positive sentiment is reflected in concrete economic indicators: business registrations increased by 23% in the past quarter, commercial lending to SMEs grew by 18%, and business investment in technology and equipment rose by 15% compared to the previous year.

Context & Analysis

The surge in business confidence follows several quarters of economic stabilization and the implementation of pro-business reforms. Government initiatives reducing bureaucratic hurdles and providing tax incentives for investment have been particularly effective in stimulating entrepreneurial activity.

Business Confidence Drivers:

  • Simplified business registration process
  • Tax incentives for small businesses
  • Improved access to export markets
  • Stable currency and controlled inflation
  • Growing consumer purchasing power

Economic Analyst's Perspective

"This optimism isn't just sentiment—it's backed by real economic improvements. Businesses are seeing increased demand, better access to financing, and a more predictable regulatory environment. The challenge will be sustaining this momentum through continued reforms and infrastructure investment."

- Victor Rosca, Economic Analyst
