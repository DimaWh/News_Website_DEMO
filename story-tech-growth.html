Tech Sector Shows Promising Growth in Q3 Reports

IT exports surge by 18% as Moldova positions itself as emerging tech hub

Digital Economy Expansion Accelerates

Chișinău - Moldova's technology sector has demonstrated remarkable growth in the third quarter of 2025, with official reports showing an 18% increase in IT exports compared to the same period last year. This performance makes technology one of the fastest-growing segments of the Moldovan economy.

The surge is attributed to increased foreign investment in local tech startups, enhanced government support for digital infrastructure, and a growing number of skilled software developers graduating from Moldovan universities. Major growth areas include cybersecurity, fintech solutions, and artificial intelligence applications.

"We're seeing unprecedented interest from international companies seeking partnerships with Moldovan tech firms," stated Elena Vasilescu, Director of the National IT Association. "Our developers' expertise is gaining global recognition, particularly in specialized fields like blockchain and machine learning."

The government's "Digital Moldova 2030" initiative has played a crucial role in this growth, providing tax incentives for tech companies and funding for digital skills training programs. Over 5,000 new tech jobs were created in the past quarter alone.

Context & Analysis

The technology sector's growth comes as part of Moldova's broader economic diversification strategy. With traditional agriculture facing climate challenges, the government has prioritized digital economy development as a key growth driver.

Growth Highlights:

  • 18% increase in IT exports year-over-year
  • 5,200 new technology jobs created in Q3
  • 45% growth in startup funding
  • 32% increase in women in tech roles
  • 15 new international tech partnerships

Technology Correspondent's Analysis

"Moldova's tech success story is built on three pillars: quality education producing skilled graduates, government policies supporting innovation, and the entrepreneurial spirit of our young developers. The challenge now is sustaining this growth through continued investment in education and infrastructure."

- Andrei Bostan, Technology Correspondent
